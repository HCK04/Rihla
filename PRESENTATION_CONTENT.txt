  a clear offline message and fall back to cached content.

Q8: "How does the camera scan feature work technically?
     How accurate is it on Moroccan food?"

ANSWER:
"We use Claude's Vision API — a multimodal AI model that was
trained on vast image-text pairs. We send the image with a
Morocco-specific system prompt that instructs it to identify
Moroccan dishes, monuments, and crafts by name, and to return
a cultural story.
Current accuracy on common dishes and famous monuments is strong.
For rare or uncommon items, it returns its best assessment
with an uncertainty note.
The accuracy improves as we add more Morocco-specific training
examples to the system prompt context."

---

Q9: "What if the AI gives wrong cultural information about Morocco?"

ANSWER:
"This is a real risk we take seriously. Our mitigation:
all AI responses are grounded in our manually curated
spots database — the AI is answering FROM our data,
not generating from scratch.
For camera scan, responses are reviewed against a whitelist
of verified Moroccan food and monument names.
We also display a disclaimer: 'AI-generated — verify with locals.'
And we have a feedback button on every AI response so
users can flag inaccuracies. That data improves the model."

---

Q10: "Why a PWA instead of a native iOS/Android app?
      Isn't PWA worse experience?"

ANSWER:
"For our use case, PWA is actually the right choice.
Our users are foreign tourists who will never browse the
Moroccan App Store to find us. But they will scan a QR code
in an airport. Installation is instant.
PWA gives us: faster time to market, one codebase for all platforms,
no App Store fees or approval delays, and home screen shortcuts.
The gap between PWA and native has almost closed by 2026 —
camera access, offline storage, push notifications are all
available in modern PWAs.
If traction justifies it, a native app is Phase 3."

---

Q11: "Your rarity score system — how do you keep it updated?
      If a hidden gem goes viral, do you downgrade it?"

ANSWER:
"Yes, exactly. The rarity score is meant to be dynamic.
As review counts increase, rarity decreases — automatically.
That's what makes it honest. A place that was a hidden gem
two years ago but is now featured in every travel magazine
should be marked as 'Known Spot', not falsely labelled rare.
Users trust the score because it reflects reality.
We update scores monthly using API data refreshes."
